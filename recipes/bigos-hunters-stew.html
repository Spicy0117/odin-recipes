Bigos "Hunters Stew"

A perfect meal for a long rest

Bigos, or Hunters Stew, a hefty strong meal to raise ones spirits on a cold winters day.

A meal to help regain stamina lost while questing throughh lands unknown.

Perfect with a fresh loaf of bread of your choosing and a barrel of the heartist mead to cheer and celebrate any victory or defeat too.

WARNINGThis meal WILL make you feel fat and sassy.

Ingredients

  • 1 lbs Kielbasa Sausage sliced into 1/2 inch pieces
  • 2 strips Thick Hickory-Smoked Bacon (Can be subbed with Ham for authenticity)
  • 1 lbs Cubed Pork meat (Can be subbed with Venison for authenticity)
  • 1/4 cup All purpose Flour
  • 4 cups Shreadded Green Cabage
  • 2 Carrots,diced
  • 1 Onions,diced
  • 1.5 cups Sliced Fresh Mushrooms
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic, diced
  • 1 Nappa Cabbage, diced
  • 2 Green Apples, diced (Optional)
  • 2 Potatoes, cubed (Optional)
  • 1/4 cup Red Wine
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 1 tbls Sweet Paprika
  • 1 tsp Dried Basil
  • 1 tsp Dried Marjoram
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1/8 tsp Craway Seed/ crushed
  • 1 big pinch Cayenne Pepper
  • 5 cups Beef Stock
  • 1 cup Canned Diced Tomatoes
  • 1/2 ounce Dried Mushrooms
  • 2 tbs Canned Tomatoe Paste
  • 1 dash Bottled Hot Pepper Sauce
  • 1 dash Worcestershire Sauce

    • Saurkraut for toppings, Bread for soaking up the juices, and Mead for drinking

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Heat a large pot over medium heat. Add kielbasa and bacon; cook and stir until bacon has rendered its fat and sausage is lightly browned. Use a slotted spoon to remove the meat and transfer to a large casserole or Dutch oven
  3. Coat cubes of pork lightly with flour and fry them in the bacon drippings over medium-high heat until golden brown. Use a slotted spoon to transfer pork to the casserole. Add cabbage, carrots, onion, potatoes, apples, fresh mushrooms, and garlic. Add in nappa cabbage. Reduce heat to medium; cook and stir until carrots are soft, about 10 minutes. Do not let the vegetables brown.
  4. Deglaze the pan by pouring in red wine and stirring to loosen all of the bits of food and flour that are stuck to the bottom. Season with bay leaf, paprika, basil, marjoram, salt, pepper, caraway seed, and cayenne; cook for 1 minute.
  5. Mix in beef stock, tomatoes, dried mushrooms, tomato paste, hot pepper sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Heat through just until boiling. Pour vegetables and all of the liquid into the casserole dish with meat. Cover with a lid.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, until meat is very tender.
  7. Serve with a side of saurkraut, fresh bread, or whatever else you like to eat with stew on a cold winters night.
